Education

Includes High School, College, and Graduate School

TIS: Tashkent International School

The most prestigious high school in Uzbekistan, TIS, is my alma mater. After graduating from my Uzbekistani local school, tasked with deciding what to do with the rest of my life, I had the choice of going to an Uzbek or Russian University or expanding my horizons and reaching for something greater. My choice was to accept a scholarship to our international high school, a place where I knew that if I succeeded, I'd have the chance to take my life and my dreams further and wider than I'd ever imagined before. So, I did just that, graduating with an A average over my two years there, and giving myself a chance at a lifetime of success.

Sewanee: University of the South

After choosing to leave Uzbekistan for college, I had to decide where to attend. I chose Sewanee because of it's beautiful campus, yes, but also because of the success of its students after graduation. I knew I wanted to take my education as far as possible, and Sewanee's reputation for helping students go on to be successful in Masters and PhD programs after graduation was more than I could pass up. With my full scholarship, I went on to graduate magna cum laude and expand my future.

GIP: Kyung Hee Graduate Institute of Peace Studies

After leaving Sewanee, a place I had come to love, and traveling the world, I decided that I wanted to move my horizons even broader and grow my understanding of how best to help the world. That's why I chose to attend GIP. I knew that, more than anything else, I wanted to help bring Peace between people in the wrold. The wide-ranging and inclusive curriculum offered there helped me see so many things about the world that I hadn't before. I dedicated myself to understanding as much about the world as I could and was able to graduated summa cum laude with the award for Most Outstanding Thesis.
