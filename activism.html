Activism

Includes Writing, Women's Center, Equal Rights

Writing

Although speaking is something I believe I have come to do well, I've always expressed myself most vividly through my writing. This includes expressing my activism. Even as a young girl, I saw how racism, sexism and hyper-nationalist views can hurt communities. My writing on matters like this has since won awards, like a UN prize for writing, but my great hope is that my writing might change minds and hearts to be more open, accepting, and inclusive of all people.

Women's Center

My great passion has always been the fight for equal rights for women around the world. That's why I was always in awe of the Bairnwick Women's center at Sewanee, my college, a university group and house dedicated to sponsoring events and information that push forward the fight for women's rights on the Sewanee campus. That's why being a leader in the work there during my time at Sewanee was so important to me. I cherished my time there and the important work we did.

Equal Rights

I've been driven to make a difference in every facet of my life and work. That's why I've made it my mission to do things like volunteer at home's for trafficked women, volunteer at HIV clinics or give my time to march for equal rights for women, LGBTQI or people of all races. The fight for social justice and equal rights if long and hard. I've made it my life's mission to be in the battle.
